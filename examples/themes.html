Why accessible design matters

More than one billion people worldwide live with some form of disability. Accessibility ensures that websites and applications can be used by everyone, regardless of their abilities or the assistive technologies they rely on.

"The power of the web is in its universality. Access by everyone regardless of disability is an essential aspect." — Tim Berners-Lee

Implementing accessibility features doesn't just help users with disabilities — it often improves the experience for all users. Larger text, higher contrast, and reduced animations make interfaces easier to use in many everyday situations.

Key principles

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) define four core principles: content must be Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, and Robust (POUR). Meeting these principles is the foundation of any inclusive web experience.
